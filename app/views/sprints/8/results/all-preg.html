You get free prescriptions and dental care

You get free prescriptions if you have a maternity exemption certificate while you're pregnant and up until a year after your child's birth.

You also get free dental care, but don't have a maternity exemption certificate, as you can use other proof.


What you'll need as proof

Prescriptions

You may have to show your maternity exemption certificate.

You can get an application form from your midwife, doctor or health visitor.

If you didn't get your maternity exemption certificate while you were pregnant, you can still apply at any time during the 12 months after your baby was born.


Dental check ups and treatment

You may need to show proof of your pregnancy, or that you have had a baby in the past 12 months. You can show things like:

  • a valid maternity exemption card
  • a MATB1 certificate issued by your midwife or GP
