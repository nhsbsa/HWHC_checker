Help for people who get benefits

You already get help with health costs if you get any of these benefits:

  • Income Support,
  • Universal Credit,
  • Income based Job Seekers Allowance,
  • Income related Employment and Support Allowance,
  • Pension Credit Guarantee Credit.

Tax Credits

You can get a Tax Credit Exemption Certificate if you have a family income of less than £15,276 and get one of these types of tax credits:

  • Working Tax Credit and Child Tax Credit together,
  • Working Tax Credit on its own, including a disability element,
  • Child Tax Credit on its own.

If you qualify, you should have been sent an NHS Tax Credit Exemption Certificate automatically, but if not, you can show the tax credit award notice as evidence to your health care provider.


Benefits that do not automatically get you help with health costs

You do not get automatic help if you get any of these benefits:

  • Working Tax Credit on its own,
  • Contribution based Job Seekers Allowance
  • contribution based Employment and Support Allowance
  • Pension Credit savings credit on its own
  • Incapacity benefit
  • Personal Independence Payment
  • Armed Forces Independence Payment
  • Disability Living Allowance
