You get free prescriptions, eye-exams and dental check ups

Because you live in Scotland you get free:

  • NHS prescriptions
    If your GP is in Scotland and your prescription is dispensed in Scotland.
  • NHS eye examinations
    If your optician is in Scotland.
  • NHS dental check ups
    If you are registered with a Scottish dentist.

    • If you live in the Highlands or Islands, you pay £10 towards the cost of your travel to hospital and the remaining amount will be refunded through the Highlands and Islands Patient Travel Scheme.

If you have a GP in England

If you live in Scotland but your GP practice is in England, you are still able to get free prescriptions.

You will need to show the pharmacist a valid Entitlement Card along with your prescription.

How to apply for an Entitlement Card

  • Get an application form from your GP practice, or
  • contact your local Practitioner Services office

You may also get help with the other costs

If you find it hard to pay your NHS costs, you could get help if your left with less than £128 after you pay your council tax and housing costs.

How much help you get depends on your household income and outgoings.

You don't have to be claiming benefits to apply.

Any help you're entitled to is also available to your partner.
