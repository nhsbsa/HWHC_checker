NHSBSA
Help with NHS costs

Children and young people

Under 16

Children under 16 get:

  • Free NHS prescriptions
  • Free NHS dental treatment
  • Free NHS Sight tests
  • Free NHS wigs and fabric supports
  • Help towards the cost of new glasses or contact lenses
  • Help towards the cost of repairing or replacing your glasses or contact lenses

Unless the personal responsible for them, usually a parent, gets certain benefits or has a low income, they don't automatically get:

  • Help towards the cost of travelling for NHS treatment

Young people aged 16,17 or 18

Those in full time education

Young people aged 16, 17 or 18 who are in full-time education get:

  • Free NHS prescriptions
  • Free NHS Sight tests
  • Free NHS wigs and fabric supports
  • Help towards the cost of new glasses or contact lenses
  • Help towards the cost of repairing or replacing your glasses or contact lenses

Unless they or the person responsible for them, usually a parent, gets certain benefits or has a low income, they don't automatically get:

  • Help towards the cost of travelling for NHS treatment

Those not in full time education

Young people aged 16, 17 or 18 who aren't in full-time education get:

  • Free NHS dental treatment for any course of treatment that starts before their 18th birthday

Unless they or the person responsible for them, usually a parent, gets certain benefits or has a low income, they don't automatically get:

  • Free NHS prescriptions
  • Free NHS Sight tests
  • Free NHS wigs and fabric supports
  • Help towards the cost of new glasses or contact lenses
  • Help towards the cost of repairing or replacing your glasses or contact lenses
  • Help towards the cost of travelling for NHS treatment

Young people aged 19

Young people aged 19 don't automatically get any help with paying NHS costs, unless:

  • They or the person responsible for them (usually a parent) has a Tax Credits Exemption Certificate and are receiving a Child Tax Credit payment for them

    • or

  • They or the person responsible for them usually a parent, gets certain benefits and they are named as dependant

Living in or getting treatment in Scotland

Regardless of age, if your GP is in Scotland and your prescription is dispensed in Scotland, you'll get:

  • Free NHS prescriptions

Regardless of age, if you are a UK resident and visit an opticians in Scotland, you'll get:

  • Free NHS eye examinations

Regardless of age, if you are registered with a Scottish dentist, you'll get:

  • Free NHS dental check ups

Living in or getting treatment in Wales

Regardless of age, if your GP is in Wales and your prescription is dispensed in Wales, you'll get:

  • Free NHS prescriptions

Up until your 25th birthday, if you are registered with a Welsh dentist, you'll get:

  • Free NHS dental check ups
